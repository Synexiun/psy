# Team & Hiring — Discipline OS

## 1. Organizational Philosophy

### What makes this company's hiring different

Four requirements every role must meet:

1. **Comfortable with emotional weight.** We ship software for people in vulnerable moments. Employees who can't sit with that are the wrong hires.
2. **Discipline to refuse category anti-patterns.** Investors will push for streaks, badges, growth hacks. Hires who cave kill the product.
3. **Long-arc patience.** 14–18 months to v1.0. 3–4 years to clinical SKU. Hires who need quarterly wins are mismatched.
4. **Strong privacy instincts.** Shortcuts with user data are the kiss of death.

### Cultural Principles

- **Outcome > activity.** Measured in retention, not sprints.
- **Honesty > politeness.** Say hard things directly. Disagree openly. Commit after decisions.
- **Depth > breadth.** Fewer things, done excellently.
- **Craft > speed.** A week of deliberate design beats two weeks of rework.
- **Humility > hero.** No one saves the day alone. Especially in this category.

- Product sense (user-first, ruthless prioritizer)

- Track record: ideally 0-1 founder before; at minimum 5+ years product leadership

### Role Required: CTO
- Senior mobile engineering (10+ years, native iOS / Android, cross-platform production)
- ML / data engineering depth
- Infrastructure / backend experience at scale
- Security + privacy mindset
- Hiring instinct (will build team of 20+ engineers)

### Role Required: Head of Clinical
- Licensed psychologist (PhD or PsyD)
- Publications in Relapse Prevention / MBRP / JITAI / CBT
- Willingness to serve full-time (or 60%+) rather than advise part-time
- Experience in digital health products (strongly preferred)

**If any founding seat is missing, solve before fundraising.** Investors in this space look for all three.

## 5. Clinical Advisory Board

### Target Composition

**3 named advisors, each with:**
- Doctoral clinical or research psychology degree
- Published in behavioral intervention research (Marlatt RP, MBRP, JITAI, CBT, DBT, or ACT lineage)
- Willingness to be publicly associated
- Commitment to quarterly reviews and product input

### Profile Targets

- Advisor 1: **Relapse Prevention / Addiction** (faculty-level)
- Advisor 2: **JITAI / Digital Health** (researcher with published methodology)
- Advisor 3: **CBT / DBT clinical practice** (clinician-researcher, patient-work grounding)

### Compensation

- $40k–80k cash + 0.25–0.5% equity over 4-year vest
- Quarterly 2-hour reviews
- Annual methodology paper co-authorship opportunity
- Public credit on site and in methodology

### Scientific Ethics

- Advisors cannot be paid on outcomes (avoids pressure to soften review)
- Advisors have veto power on clinical claims (contract right)
- Advisors can publicly dissent (contract right; this is a feature, not a bug)
- Advisors' academic work is never gated by company approval

### Recruitment

- CEO + Head of Clinical personally reach out
- First ask is 30-min chat, not advisory ask
- Show methodology work already done before formal advisory ask
- Reference other digital-health advisory structures they respect (Pear, Akili for structure, not products)

## 6. Compensation Philosophy

### Principles

- **Cash competitive at 50th–75th percentile** for role + location + stage
- **Equity generous for first 20 hires**, standard for later
- **No fixed bonus schemes** tied to vanity metrics
- **Performance reviews semi-annual**, not annual
- **Transparency:** all employees know compensation bands for their role

### Equity Framework

- Total employee option pool at seed: 15%
- Refreshed to 12% after Series A (expansion)
- 4-year vest, 1-year cliff standard
- Early-exercise allowed for senior hires (tax-advantaged)
- 10-year post-termination exercise window (not 90 days) — employee-friendly

### Benefits (US)

- Full medical / dental / vision (100% for employee, 70% family)
- Mental health coverage (meta-level: we practice what we preach)
- 25-day PTO + company-wide close-downs
- Parental leave: 16 weeks primary, 8 weeks secondary
- Remote-first with quarterly offsites
- $2k home-office stipend
- $1.5k/yr continuous learning budget

## 7. Remote / Hybrid Structure

### Default: Remote-first, timezone-concentrated

- Core hours: 10am–3pm Eastern US (5-hour overlap across US timezones)
- Quarterly in-person offsites (4–5 days)
- Regional hubs where 3+ team members cluster (informal gather space)

### Hires located outside US

- Acceptable via EOR (Deel, Remote.com, Oyster)
- Salary parity-adjusted (not below 75% of US equivalent)
- UK and EU hires from Y2 for local regulatory and GTM

## 8. What We Will Not Do

- Hire generalists for roles that need specialists
- Hire growth-hackers (our GTM doesn't have that job)
- Outsource core engineering (security + IP)
- Outsource clinical content (quality risk)
- Tolerate performative culture (wellness theater)
- Accept cultural misalignment "because they're great at the job"

## 9. Performance & Accountability

### Goal Setting

- Company: 3 annual objectives, 10–12 key results
- Team: quarterly OKRs mapped to company
- Individual: 2–4 focus areas per half, reviewed every 2 months

### Performance Review

- Semi-annual: full review, 360 feedback
- Monthly 1:1s between every manager and direct report
- Quarterly: skip-level 1:1s
- Annual: compensation + level review

### Underperformance

- First sign: direct conversation + plan
- No silent non-renewal or ghost-PIP
- If PIP, it's 60 days with specific goals — and honest either way
- Severance: 6 weeks minimum + unvested option extension for clear terminations

## 11. Hiring for Specific Risks

Because this is an unusual product, we deliberately screen for:

- **Designer candidates:** Show work for crisis-state flows. Can they design when the user can't think?
- **Engineer candidates:** Ask about time they said "no" to a PM pushing feature bloat.
- **Product candidates:** Have they held a retention metric as a primary KPI?
- **Growth candidates:** Have they declined a CAC-positive channel because of quality? (If "no, I'd take any positive channel" — wrong hire.)
- **Clinical candidates:** Have they worked with product teams? (Academic-only → slower).
- **Sales candidates:** Can they sell without promising efficacy we can't back?

## 12. Onboarding

- Week 1: Product philosophy, methodology, privacy/safety training (mandatory, clinical-advisor-designed)
- Week 2: Codebase / system walkthrough by peer
- Week 3: First meaningful contribution (shippable)
- Week 4: Retrospective and feedback both ways
- 90-day review: deep check-in, adjust role if needed

Clinical + safety training is mandatory for every employee, not just clinical team. Engineers who understand Marlatt's RP write better code.

---

## 13. Summary

Hiring for Discipline OS is hiring for a company where:
- The work matters more than the title
- User outcomes are the scoreboard
- Discipline (yes, even internally) is the culture
- Patience is a virtue, urgency is reserved for user-facing work
- Clinical integrity is non-negotiable

If we hire well, we win. If we hire for pattern-match against generic startup archetypes, we fail. The third condition in the research report — *"discipline to refuse category anti-patterns"* — lives or dies with the people around the table.
