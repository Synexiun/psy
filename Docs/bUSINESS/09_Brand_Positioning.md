# Brand & Positioning — Discipline OS

## 1. Brand Essence

### What we are
**The thing that's there at the moment it counts.**

Not a tracker. Not a coach. Not a program. A presence — specifically, at the 60–180 seconds between urge and action where everything else fails.

### What we stand for
**Self-trust.** Not transformation. Not optimization. Not being "fixed." Becoming someone you can rely on.

### What we reject
- Transformation marketing ("new you")
- Shame-adjacent language ("failed," "broken," "weak")
- Urgency manipulation
- Gamified triviality
- AI hype

---

## 2. Brand Principles

1. **Honest about hard things.** Users in our category can smell performative empathy. We never sugarcoat, never manipulate, never promise what we can't deliver.
2. **Clinical without being cold.** We know the science. We also know you're a person, not a patient case number.
3. **Specific, not vague.** "4 of 5 urges handled this week" beats "great progress!" Every time.
4. **Compassionate about relapse.** Relapse is data. It's never failure. Our copy reflects this in every word.
5. **Serious about craft.** The app's clinical depth is visible in design quality. Shoddy visuals betray shoddy thinking.

---

## 3. Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Direct** without being blunt
- **Warm** without being saccharine
- **Informed** without being clinical-cold
- **Confident** without being cocky

### Example Comparisons

**Bad (shame-adjacent):**
> "Don't give up! You've made it 12 days — don't break your streak!"

**Better (category-typical, still off):**
> "You're doing great! Keep going — your future self will thank you!"

**Discipline OS voice:**
> "12 days handled. Today's a tougher one based on your patterns — want a 2-minute tool before 5pm?"

---

**Bad (fake urgency):**
> "Limited time! Upgrade now to Pro!"

**Discipline OS voice:**
> "Pro gives you the pattern analytics and personalized coaching. Free keeps the tools that matter. Either is fine."

---

**Bad (after a relapse, competitor-typical):**
> "Your streak has been reset. Start again today!"

**Discipline OS voice:**
> "That's hard. You're still here — that matters. Want to look at what happened together when you're ready?"

## 5. Visual Direction

### Aesthetic Target
**Clinical-modern.** Serious. Calm. Confident. Not warm-and-fuzzy (Finch, Calm). Not austere-corporate (Intellect). Something closer to **Linear + Oura + Anthropic**.

### Color Palette

**Primary:**
- Graphite #1A1D23 (primary text, UI anchors)
- Off-white #F7F7F5 (background default)
- Signal Blue #2E5FFF (primary actions, state indicators)

**Secondary:**
- Slate #5A6374 (secondary text)
- Mist #E8EAEE (surfaces, dividers)
- Deep #0F1117 (dark mode anchor)

**State (used minimally):**
- Calm #6FB3B8 (low-state indicator)
- Elevated #E8A85B (elevated state — never red)
- Crisis #D14B3E (T3/T4 only — reserved)

**Never used:**
- Bright reds (shame-coded)
- Pinks, pastels (Finch territory)
- Neon accent colors (gimmick coding)

### Typography

**Display:** Inter Display or Söhne — confident, modern, high legibility
**Body:** Inter — universal, accessible
**Clinical / code:** JetBrains Mono (methodology pages, technical docs)

### Imagery

**Do:**
- Abstract geometric compositions (state visualizations)
- Real humans captured candidly (consent-driven portraits of users + advisors)
- Data-forward diagrams

**Don't:**
- Stock photography of smiling people
- Sunrise / mountain-peak metaphors
- Before/after transformation imagery
- Hands-holding-phone shots
- Fake screenshots with "amazing results"

### Iconography

- Rounded-corner geometric icons (2px stroke)
- Minimal, functional
- Custom set (not Material or iOS defaults)

### Motion

- Restrained. Never "delightful" for its own sake.
- Crisis flows: **zero animation** (reduces cognitive load)
- Dashboard: subtle state transitions
- Onboarding: one carefully designed moment of motion per screen, maximum

## 6. Naming

### Product Name Options

**Candidate 1: "Discipline OS"** (working name)
- Pros: Category-creating, memorable, aspirational
- Cons: "OS" terminology may feel cold; "Discipline" may feel austere

**Candidate 2: "Axis"**
- Pros: Short, metaphor-rich (pivot point between urge and action)
- Cons: Crowded name, harder to trademark broadly

**Candidate 3: "Keystone"**
- Pros: Architectural metaphor, calm authority
- Cons: Can feel old-fashioned

**Candidate 4: "Tether"**
- Pros: Emotional resonance, implies connection back to self
- Cons: Crypto namespace collision

**Candidate 5: "Pivot"**
- Pros: Names the exact moment
- Cons: Generic business-speak

**Candidate 6: "Ground"**
- Pros: Psychological grounding double-meaning, calm
- Cons: Common word, trademark harder

**Recommendation:** Start with **"Discipline OS"** in private beta, test 3 names with beta users at Month 10, decide before v1.0 launch.

### Company vs. Product

Could be separate:
- **Product name:** user-facing (aspirational / emotional)
- **Company name:** investor / enterprise-facing (credible / structured)

Example: Product "Axis" · Company "Discipline Labs, Inc."

### Alt App Icon / Name for Privacy

For users who need to hide usage:
- Icon options: generic "Reflect" / "Compass" / "Notes" / "Moments"
- Launchable from Settings → Privacy → Alternative app presentation

## 8. Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Publish methodology openly
- Name clinical advisors with credentials
- Use specific outcome numbers (including misses)
- Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly when asked
- Respond to user complaints substantively (not with canned empathy)
- Share product decisions that went wrong, publicly, with learning
- Lead the industry on privacy standards

### Don't
- Fabricate testimonials or cherry-pick outcomes
- Claim to "cure" anything
- Use "scientifically proven" loosely
- Attack competitors publicly
- Respond to criticism defensively
- Manipulate urgency or scarcity
- Position on technology ("AI coach") over outcome
- Target minors
- Exploit vulnerable moments in marketing

## 9. Content Pillars (Editorial Strategy)

Every piece falls into one of five pillars:

1. **Science pillar** — Plain-language explainers of behavioral research. Establishes credibility.
2. **Method pillar** — How Discipline OS works. Establishes differentiation.
3. **Story pillar** — User outcomes (consented, specific). Establishes trust.
4. **Category pillar** — "What is a Behavioral OS?" Defines new category.
5. **Responsibility pillar** — Privacy, ethics, limitations. Establishes integrity.

### Target: 1 major piece / week; 1 piece per pillar per month.

## 10. Crisis Communications Posture

### Incident Response Principles

1. **Speed and honesty over polish.** A 12-hour rough statement beats a 5-day polished one.
2. **Clinical advisor visibility** on any incident involving user harm.
3. **Never blame the user.** Even if technically accurate, the brand loses.
4. **Post-mortem published** within 30 days of resolution.
5. **Specific remediations** named with timelines.

### Playbook (Brief)

**Data incident:** CEO statement within 24h; user notification within 72h per GDPR; post-mortem within 30 days.

**T4 adverse outcome:** Clinical advisor statement; private outreach to affected parties; classifier review; public acknowledgment if pattern.

**Negative press:** Factual response, one round only. Don't feed cycles.

**Competitor attack:** Ignore unless materially false. Then one factual correction.

---

## 11. The Brand Test

Every brand decision passes this test: **Would this survive scrutiny by a thoughtful user in their most vulnerable moment?**

If a piece of copy, a notification, a design choice, or a marketing claim feels wrong when a user is at peak urge — kill it. The category is unforgiving. Everything that survives that test becomes the moat.
