## 6. Sensitivity Analysis

### Downside (60% of base)

- Consumer paid users Y5: 660k (vs 1.1M base)
- Enterprise contracts Y5: 240 (vs 400)
- No clinical SKU until Y5
- Y5 ARR: $99M, EBITDA: $38M
- Still a viable business; exit option at 6–8x ARR

### Upside (130% of base)

- Consumer paid users Y5: 1.4M
- Enterprise contracts Y5: 520 with avg ACV $190k
- Clinical SKU lives Y5: 70k
- Y5 ARR: $214M
- Series B optional; path to IPO in Y7–Y8

### Key Sensitivities

| Variable | -1σ impact on Y5 ARR | +1σ impact |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Consumer D90 retention | -$22M | +$19M |
| Enterprise ACV | -$14M | +$16M |
| Clinical SKU timing (± 12 mo) | -$10M | +$8M |
| Paid conversion rate | -$18M | +$15M |
| Monthly churn rate | -$25M | +$21M |

**Top finding:** monthly churn is the single most sensitive variable. Every 0.5 percentage point reduction in monthly churn adds ~$8M to Y5 ARR. Retention isn't just important — it's the dominant financial lever.

## 7. Financial Risks

1. **Retention shortfall (critical).** Consumer churn 1 point higher than plan costs $10M+ by Y5. Mitigation: retention is the primary Q1–Q4 metric.
2. **Clinical SKU slip (high).** 12-month delay shifts $13M of Y5 revenue. Mitigation: clinical track begins Y1, wellness path doesn't depend on it.
3. **Enterprise slower than plan (high).** Sales cycles longer than benefits research. Mitigation: start pilots Month 14 not Month 20.
4. **Apple / Google commoditization (medium).** Could pressure consumer ARPU. Mitigation: enterprise and clinical streams (harder to commoditize).
5. **CAC inflation (medium).** Paid social costs continue rising. Mitigation: founder-content and organic channels are our anchor.
6. **Regulatory shock (low but high impact).** Adverse FDA or FTC ruling. Mitigation: conservative wellness claims, pre-clearance from day 1 in messaging.

## 9. Summary

Discipline OS is a capital-efficient business by category standards:
- Seed round gets us to paid beta + near-launch
- Series A funds commercial ramp with unit economics already proven
- Path to profitability by late Y3
- Enterprise + clinical diversification mean the business has multiple valid exit structures (acquisition at Y3–Y4 at 8–12x ARR, Series B + IPO path at Y5–Y8)

**The financial story is simple:** retention-first design produces compounding unit economics that every competitor leaves on the table. Hold that discipline and the numbers take care of themselves.
