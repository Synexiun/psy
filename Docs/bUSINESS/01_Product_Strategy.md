# Product Strategy — Discipline OS

## 1. Vision (5-Year Horizon)

> **To build the first behavioral operating system for humans — infrastructure that turns the gap between intention and action into measurable, learnable, compassionate practice.**

In five years, Discipline OS is:
- The default intervention layer for impulse control across consumer, enterprise, and clinical markets
- The category-naming product for "behavioral operating system" (as Calm was for meditation)
- A prescribable FDA-cleared digital therapeutic for multiple indications (alcohol use disorder, anxiety, binge eating)
- A cross-vertical learned model no single-vertical competitor can replicate

---

## 2. Mission (Operating Principle)

> **To be present at the moment that matters — and to make that moment winnable.**

Every feature, every pixel, every notification earns its place by answering: *does this make the moment of urge more winnable?* If not, it doesn't ship.

---

## 3. North Star Metric

**WUH — Weekly Urges Handled per active user.**

Why not days clean, DAU, or streaks?
- **Days clean** is sensitive to definition, shame-adjacent, and punishes relapse.
- **DAU** rewards open-rate over outcome — fatal in this category.
- **Streaks** trigger AVE churn.

WUH measures *delivered intervention value*. A user with many urges handled is a user getting real utility. A user with zero urges recorded is either fully stabilized (great) or disengaged (action signal).

**Secondary metrics:**
- D30 / D90 / D180 retention
- Resilience streak growth (urges handled / week)
- Intervention efficacy (user-rated + objective outcome)
- Paid conversion rate
- Crisis escalation precision (T4 triggered when clinically warranted)

---

## 4. Strategic Pillars

Five pillars. Every quarterly plan maps to one of these.

### Pillar 1 — The Moment of Decision
Own the 30–180 seconds where the urge crests.

Key bets:
- Sub-second latency on T3 crisis path
- Native widget / watch surface for <2-tap invocation
- On-device ML for urge-trajectory prediction
- Reduced-choice UI at peak urge (1 button, 1 path)

### Pillar 2 — Relapse Done Right
Make relapse day the product's best day, not its churn day.

Key bets:
- Dual-metric dashboard (continuous + resilience)
- Enforced 24–72h compassion protocol
- Functional-analysis workflow post-lapse (not shame)
- Never lock sobriety progress as paywall hostage

### Pillar 3 — Cross-Vertical Depth
One learned model across cravings, procrastination, panic, emotional reactivity.

Key bets:
- Unified signal / state schema from day one
- Population priors seeded from single-vertical data
- Per-user contextual bandit with cross-vertical features
- Avoid single-vertical branding (blocks expansion)

### Pillar 4 — Clinical Credibility
Not theater — published methodology + advisors with peer-reviewed track records.

Key bets:
- Clinical advisory board (3 named members, RP/MBRP/JITAI publication records)
- Annual methodology paper (pre-print acceptable)
- Parallel clinical SKU from Year 2 (FDA SaMD path)
- Outcomes data published (consented, aggregated)

### Pillar 5 — Privacy as Product
Trust is the irreplaceable asset in this category.

Key bets:
- Edge ML (raw biometric never leaves device)
- E2E encryption for voice journals
- Data minimization at ingestion (pseudonymization before storage)
- User-controlled retention (delete-my-data, quick-erase, hidden-from-recents)
- No data licensing, ever (explicit line)

## 6. Category Creation Strategy

We are creating the category "**Behavioral OS**" — sitting between habit trackers (shallow) and digital therapeutics (clinical-only).

Tactics:
1. **Name the category first.** Every piece of marketing reinforces the name.
2. **Define the category's criteria.** Publish "What is a Behavioral OS?" early. Force competitors to respond in our vocabulary.
3. **Analyst placement.** Get listed in a new Gartner / Forrester / IDC category within 24 months.
4. **Academic co-authorship.** A peer-reviewed paper coining "Behavioral Operating System" in a psychology or HCI venue.
5. **Taxonomy pressure.** Journalism mentions category with our product as anchor → pressure on app stores to create new category.

**Key risk:** Apple or Google claims the name first. Move fast on category branding.

## 8. Strategic Non-Goals (What We Explicitly Don't Do)

Every "don't" below is a 10x focus multiplier.

- **We don't compete on tracking depth.** Daylio and Bearable do it better. We log what intervention needs, nothing more.
- **We don't build community at launch.** AA/NA/SMART have 80+ year moats. We integrate with them, not replace.
- **We don't gamify.** Streaks, badges, leaderboards — the evidence is clear.
- **We don't sell data.** Not aggregated, not anonymized, not "for research." Full stop.
- **We don't target minors at launch.** Separate design, separate regulatory posture.
- **We don't claim to treat diagnosed disorders** until we have clinical-SKU clearance.
- **We don't ship without wearable integration.** Phone-only undermines state estimation.
- **We don't optimize for install count.** D30 > installs.

## 9. Competitive Moats

Ranked by defensibility:

1. **Cross-vertical learned model.** Each vertical we support makes the model better at all others. Single-vertical competitors cannot replicate.
2. **Clinical advisory + methodology publication.** 18–36 months to rebuild.
3. **Outcomes data.** Published efficacy numbers become a referral magnet.
4. **Wearable integration depth.** 24+ months of engineering to match.
5. **Privacy architecture.** Edge ML + E2E is structurally hard for competitors who built cloud-first.
6. **Category brand.** First-mover on "Behavioral OS" naming.
7. **Enterprise + clinical relationships.** Sticky contracts create switching cost.

## 10. Strategic Decisions the Founder Must Make

These are not engineering decisions. They are founder decisions.

1. **Wedge vertical.** Recommended: alcohol. Alternatives: porn (high engagement, harder brand), procrastination (lower willingness to pay, harder clinical path).
2. **Geography.** Recommended: US-first. Alt: parallel US + UK (GDPR/DPA overhead but broader market).
3. **Regulatory posture year 1.** Recommended: wellness SKU public, clinical path internal-only. Alt: delay all clinical claims to Year 3.
4. **Enterprise timing.** Recommended: pilot conversations month 12, contracts month 18+. Alt: consumer-only through Year 2.
5. **Clinician channel.** Recommended: launch month 24. Alt: defer to Year 3.
6. **Community features.** Recommended: none until Year 2. Alt: opt-in peer-match at v1.5.
7. **Open vs. closed model.** Recommended: closed consumer + closed clinical, opened API in Year 5. Alt: open SDK day 1 (rejected — too much surface area early).

## 12. Summary

Discipline OS is a strategic bet on three hypotheses:

1. **Cross-vertical intervention outperforms single-vertical tracking.** (High confidence)
2. **Relapse-UX redesign beats competitor retention by 10x.** (Medium confidence — needs validation)
3. **Clinical credibility compounds into durable enterprise/clinical revenue.** (High confidence)

If any of the three is wrong, the business still works as a consumer product — it just becomes a $30M ARR business instead of a $200M one. That makes it a better bet than most consumer subscription plays: the downside is a solid business, the upside is category-defining.
