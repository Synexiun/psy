# Go-To-Market Strategy — Discipline OS

## 1. GTM Philosophy

**We do not optimize for installs. We optimize for D30 retention and paid conversion.**

Every competitor in this category has figured out how to drive installs. None have figured out retention. Our GTM is built around the discipline to under-invest in top-of-funnel while over-investing in the experience that keeps users.

Three rules:
1. **Spend is gated by retention data.** No paid acquisition until D30 retention > 18% on organic.
2. **Channel truth-testing.** Every channel proven to CAC/LTV positive before scaling.
3. **Brand > growth hacks.** In a trust-dependent category, brand compounds, hacks decay.

## 2. Launch Sequence

### Phase 0 — Mechanical-Turk Validation (Months 1–3)

- Zero app, SMS + human coach-in-loop
- 50 alcohol-reduction users
- 8-week structured study
- Measurement: self-reported urge escalation reduction, retention, willingness-to-pay
- **Gate:** Statistically meaningful urge-reduction vs. control. If yes → build. If no → redesign.

### Phase 1 — Private Alpha (Months 6–9)

- Invite-only, 150 users
- Single vertical (alcohol)
- Friends of founders + 3 paid cohort (recruited from Reframe, r/stopdrinking)
- Free access, feedback-intensive
- Measurement: engagement, tool efficacy, NPS
- **Gate:** D30 engagement >50%, tool efficacy >60% helpful rating

### Phase 2 — Closed Paid Beta (Months 10–14)

- Waitlist → 500 paying users at $9/mo beta pricing
- Vertical: alcohol
- US only
- Measurement: D30 >25%, D90 >15%, paid retention >50%
- **Gate:** Hit retention targets → v1.0 launch. Miss → extended beta.

### Phase 3 — v1.0 Public Launch (Months 14–18)

- App Store launch (US)
- Product Hunt launch
- Founder podcast tour
- Paid acquisition begins (strictly budgeted)
- **Target:** 15k paid users by month 18

### Phase 4 — Expansion (Months 18–30)

- Cross-vertical additions (procrastination, panic, emotional reactivity)
- UK + EU launch
- First enterprise pilots (3–5)
- Android feature parity if launched iOS-first

## 3. Wedge Market Strategy

### Why Alcohol as Wedge

1. **Highest willingness-to-pay** among single verticals
2. **Most developed digital-therapeutic reimbursement pathway** (AUD specifically)
3. **Large addressable audience** (~28M US adults with concerning drinking patterns)
4. **Clear outcome metric** (reduction in heavy drinking days, or abstinence)
5. **Existing community infrastructure** (AA, SMART, Reframe) means aware audience
6. **Clinical advisor availability** — more researchers in AUD than other verticals

### Why Not:
- **Porn:** high engagement but brand complexity, ad-channel restrictions, community shame
- **Procrastination:** lower willingness-to-pay, harder clinical path
- **Anxiety:** crowded with Wysa/Intellect/Woebot
- **Eating disorders:** clinical complexity + regulatory sensitivity higher than wedge should carry

### Expansion Gate

Do not expand cross-vertical until **D90 single-vertical retention exceeds 25%**. This is non-negotiable.

### Channel Strategy

- Founder content: 1 major podcast / week + 1 long-form piece / week
- SEO content: 3 cornerstone pieces per month (alcohol-reduction keywords)

**Hold channels (not launched until data):**

- TikTok / influencer

**Rejected channels:**
- Affiliate programs paying per signup (adverse selection into vulnerable populations)
- Lead-gen agencies
- App Store interstitial ads

### Content Strategy

**Cornerstone content types:**
1. **The Science library** — plain-language explainers of Marlatt RP, JITAI, implementation intentions, AVE, urge surfing. Positions us as clinical-credible.
2. **Founder essays** — thesis-level writing on why this product, why now. Builds trust.
3. **User outcome stories** (with explicit consent) — narrative, not clinical.
4. **"What a Behavioral OS is"** — category-defining content.

**Cadence:** 1 major piece per week, syndicated across blog / newsletter / social.

## 7. Brand & Messaging in Market

### Voice Principles

1. **Clinically credible, not clinical-cold.** We know our science. We're also kind.
2. **Honest about hard things.** Never sugarcoat. Users in our category can smell BS.
3. **Specific, not vague.** "4 of 5 urges handled this week" beats "great progress!"
4. **No shame, ever.** Not in copy, not in UI, not in marketing.

### Message Hierarchy

**Hero message:** "The moment between the urge and the action."
**Subhead:** "A behavioral operating system that's there when it counts — and learns from every try."
**Proof:** Clinical advisors named, methodology linked, outcomes published.

### Channels & Creative

- **Landing page:** long-form, honest, methodology-forward
- **Onboarding emails:** 5-email sequence pre-install (from waitlist) + 7-email sequence post-install
- **Notification copy:** always compassion-first ("Noticed your energy's low — quick check-in?")
- **Ad creative:** founder-first, no stock photos, no before/after manipulation

### Brand Don'ts

- No "transformation" before/after images
- No "you've been lied to" conspiracy-tone copy (common in alcohol-reduction marketing)
- No fake urgency ("limited time offer on recovery!")
- No testimonial fabrication or curation bias
- No "AI will save you" hype

## 9. Retention-First GTM Principles

Retention drives LTV. Every GTM choice asks: **does this improve retention or degrade it?**

- **Refund policy:** full refund, no questions, first 30 days. A refunded user is a brand fan; a kept-through-friction user churns in 60 days anyway.
- **Cancellation:** one-tap, no retention flows, no discount-save offers. Exit interview optional.
- **Win-back:** lapsed users get a single thoughtful re-engagement email after 60 days and again at 180. No more.
- **Downgrade > cancel:** always offer Free tier as a downgrade option.

## 12. What We Will Not Do to Grow

- Pay for installs without proving retention first
- Buy email lists
- Run "free trial with auto-convert" dark pattern subscriptions
- Hide cancel flows
- Manipulate testimonials or outcomes data
- Partner with influencers without content review
- Target minors
- Advertise in contexts that exploit vulnerable moments

The absence of these tactics is itself GTM — trust becomes the differentiator.
