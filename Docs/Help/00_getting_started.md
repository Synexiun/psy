# Getting started

Welcome. This is a short guide to what your first day with Discipline OS looks like, and what each step is for.

## What this app is

Discipline OS is a behavioral support tool. It is designed to help in the moments when an urge or impulse is pulling you away from what you actually want. It is not therapy, and it is not a replacement for a clinician. If you are working with a therapist or a program, this app is designed to complement that, not compete with it.

## Onboarding — what we ask, and why

When you open the app for the first time, you will go through a short onboarding. It takes about ten minutes. Here is what happens and why.

### 1. What you want to work on

You will pick the behavior you are trying to change. At launch, the product focuses on alcohol; other areas are coming.

You will also choose a goal: reduce, abstain, or maintain. There is no "right" answer — your goal is your goal.

### 2. Permissions

We will ask for a few permissions. Each one is optional. You can turn any of them off at any time.

- **Health data** (Apple Health or Health Connect): lets the app see your heart rate, sleep, and other health signals. These help the app notice patterns before you do. **Raw health data never leaves your phone.**
- **Notifications:** lets the app reach you with brief, well-timed check-ins.
- **Location** (optional): the app only uses a *category* of where you are (home, work, a place you've flagged as risky) — never your address.

### 3. Baseline

You will answer a few short questions about your history, what situations tend to be difficult, and what support you have. This helps the app know where to start. Nothing here is shared; nothing here is a test.

### 4. Your first if-then plan

You will write one plan, in this shape: *"If X happens, I will do Y."* Research shows that writing this down, once, in advance, makes it measurably more likely you will do Y in the moment. It takes two minutes. It is probably the most useful two minutes in onboarding.

### 5. Your first coping tool

You will try box breathing — a 90-second exercise that lowers arousal fast. This is a low-friction first tool. There are more.

### 6. Check-in cadence

You choose when the app checks in — morning, evening, or both. You can skip any check-in without penalty.

## The dashboard

Your dashboard has three views:

- **Today:** where you are right now and what might help.
- **Patterns:** what situations tend to bring urges for you, and which tools have worked best.
- **Trajectory:** two streaks. A *continuous streak* (days since your last lapse) and a *resilience streak* (urges you have handled). Resilience is the one that matters most, because it measures what you are actually building.

## What to expect in your first week

- The app is learning your patterns. Predictions will get better with data.
- Check-ins will feel frequent at first; the app learns when they are useful and backs off.
- You will probably have an urge at some point in the first week. That is the moment this app was built for. Tap SOS, or let a notification arrive — try the tool it suggests.

## What to do if you have a hard day

Tap SOS. Or tap "I lapsed" if that is what happened. There is no wrong way to use this app in a hard moment. The worst thing that can happen is that you open it — the system is designed so that opening is always the right move.

And if something is more than the app can handle — thoughts of self-harm, a crisis that feels beyond a coping tool — go to [Safety resources](13_safety_resources.md). Every local emergency number and crisis line is one tap away.
