# 4-7-8 breathing

4-7-8 is a shorter, steeper version of paced breathing. It is 60 seconds, and it is particularly good at dropping your arousal level fast.

## The pattern

Each cycle:

1. Breathe **in** through your nose for 4 seconds.
2. **Hold** for 7 seconds.
3. Breathe **out** through your mouth for 8 seconds.

Four cycles is about a minute. That is usually enough.

## Why the asymmetry

A breath cycle is not neutral. **Inhaling activates your sympathetic (stress) nervous system** slightly; **exhaling activates your parasympathetic (calming) nervous system** slightly. When your exhale is substantially longer than your inhale, the net effect is a shift toward calm.

4-7-8 takes this further than [box breathing](04_box_breathing.md). The 7-second hold gives the breath time to settle fully in; the 8-second exhale is where the actual down-regulation happens.

The exhale through your mouth, with a slight pursed-lip, is also helpful — it slows the release and gives you something to do with the breath.

## When to use it

- Before sleep, to wind the nervous system down.
- After a stressful event, to come back to baseline.
- When an urge is elevated but you have a full minute.
- Paired with [body scan](08_body_scan.md) as a longer settling practice.

## When not to use it

- If you are doing any physical activity — it is a stationary tool.
- If you have pulmonary conditions that make the 7-second hold uncomfortable. Use [box breathing](04_box_breathing.md) with shorter holds instead, or skip the hold entirely.
- As a crisis tool — it is slow enough that higher-intensity tools may suit better.

## Tips

- **Lips pursed slightly on the exhale.** The air should flow out steadily, not in a rush.
- **Count slowly.** If you feel rushed, make each count a full second.
- **Tongue tip on the ridge behind your upper front teeth.** This is the classic posture from the tradition this technique came out of; it helps structure the mouth for the exhale.
- **If four cycles is not enough, do two more.** The number of cycles is not sacred. What matters is that you feel a shift.

## What you will notice

Compared to box breathing, 4-7-8 tends to produce a stronger immediate drop in arousal. Some people feel slightly lightheaded on the first cycle — that is the holds doing their work. If it gets uncomfortable, shorten the holds. The tool is yours to calibrate.

## A note

4-7-8 is widely attributed to Dr. Andrew Weil's popularization in the 1990s, drawing on pranayama techniques from yoga traditions that are thousands of years old. The specific ratios are one of many valid patterns. What makes them work is the exhale-longer-than-inhale principle, which is well-established in both the autonomic physiology literature and in clinical anxiety treatment guidelines.
