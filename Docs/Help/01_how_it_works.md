# How Discipline OS works — the four tiers of support

Discipline OS does not treat every moment the same. A quiet Tuesday afternoon is not a crisis, and a 6pm Friday at a bar is not a quiet Tuesday. The system uses four tiers of support, each with a different kind of presence.

## T0 — Ambient

The app is running in the background, doing almost nothing visible. A soft indicator on your home screen shows your current state. No notifications. No interruptions.

This is most of the time. It should be.

## T1 — A gentle nudge

Something in your signals — sleep, heart rate variability, time of day, how you are using your phone — suggests your state is drifting. The app might send a short push notification. Not a warning. Just: *"quick 60 seconds?"*

You can open it. You can ignore it. If you ignore two in a row in the same context, the app stops offering that context for a week. It should learn when it is helpful to you and when it is not.

There is a daily budget, defaulting to four nudges a day. You can raise or lower this in settings.

## T2 — A tool, when an urge is climbing

When an urge is elevated but not yet at peak, the app offers a structured 90- to 180-second tool — breathing, a brief grounding exercise, a cognitive-defusion script. The tool is picked by your own history: what has worked for *you* in *this* kind of moment before. A small amount of exploration happens here, so the system keeps learning what works.

T2 can be user-initiated (you tap "I am having an urge") or system-initiated (the state estimator predicts one is coming).

## T3 — SOS

The crisis button. Full-screen. One main button. No menus. The fastest path from tap to first action in the whole app — under 800 milliseconds cold, under 200 milliseconds warm.

At T3 the app uses the single best-known tool for you in this context. There is no exploration here. There is no A/B test. Every user in every locale gets the same principle: the highest-confidence tool, immediately.

The T3 catalog is stored *on your phone*. If your internet is down, SOS still works. This is on purpose.

## T4 — When it is more than an urge

Some moments are beyond what a coping tool can hold. Thoughts of self-harm. A crisis that feels out of scale with what a breathing exercise can fix. The app does not try to handle those. What it does is bring you directly to the help that can — the crisis line for your country, the local emergency number, a contact you pre-configured, and plain supportive language that was reviewed by a clinician.

The app will not make a call for you. That choice is yours. What we can do is make sure the numbers you need are one tap away and correct for where you are.

If you want to read more, see [Safety resources](13_safety_resources.md).

## How the tiers work together

You do not need to know what tier you are in. The app does. What you will notice is that:

- Most days, nothing interrupts you.
- Sometimes, a short check-in arrives when you might benefit from one.
- When an urge rises, a tool is ready.
- When a crisis hits, the app is already there, on the lock screen, in the widget, on the watch.

It is designed to match the intensity of the moment. That is the whole point.

## What does *not* happen

- The app does not share data with your phone's advertising identifier or any ad network.
- The app does not tell anyone you have used SOS. Not your contacts, not your clinician unless you have explicitly set that up with a scope you chose.
- The app does not shame you. There is no copy in this product that says *"don't lose your progress"* or *"you broke your streak."* We designed those out on purpose.
