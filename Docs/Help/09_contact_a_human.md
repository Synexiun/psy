# Contact-a-human

Sometimes the right tool is another person.

Not a coping exercise. Not a breathing technique. A voice, or a text, or a presence. Discipline OS is a very good tool in a very specific way, and part of using it well is knowing when what you need is outside the app.

## The idea

One of the most consistent findings in the addiction and relapse-prevention literature is that **social connection in the vulnerable moment changes the trajectory**. A single text, a short call, the knowledge that someone is on the other end — these have measurable effects on whether an urge is acted on.

But the friction of reaching out in the hardest moment is exactly when friction is highest. Your phone feels heavy. You do not know what to say. You do not want to *bother* anyone. You are embarrassed.

Contact-a-human is designed to reduce that friction to the minimum.

## How it works

You configure one to three people in advance — people who have agreed to be on your list.

For each contact you save:

- **Name and phone number** (or whatever way of reaching them they prefer).
- **Pre-written message templates** that you can edit. Something like *"Hey, rough moment — can we talk for a sec?"* or *"I'm not doing great tonight. Nothing urgent; just wanted you to know."*
- A **note** about what they are for. One contact might be for everyday support; another might be for the harder moments; a sponsor; a clinician.

In a hard moment, one tap goes to your contact list. One more tap chooses a person. One more sends the message you pre-wrote.

Three taps.

## Who to ask

The best people for this list are usually:

- **Someone who has said yes to being on this list.** Do not add someone who does not know. This is not a surprise enrollment.
- **Someone who has some reliability.** Not "the person I wish would be there." The person who actually will.
- **Not the person whose approval you are trying to earn.** If reaching out to them would feel like performance, they are probably not the right pick for this.
- **Someone who knows what you are working on.** They do not need to know every detail. They need to know that when you reach out with one of these messages, something real is happening.

If you do not have anyone who fits this description, that is important information. It does not mean you are unsupported forever, but it is worth knowing now, not in the moment.

## A note on sponsors and clinicians

If you are in a 12-step program or have a sponsor, a sponsor is often a strong choice for this list. The contact-a-human flow is designed to work alongside existing support structures, not to replace them.

If you have a clinician and they have agreed to emergency contact, similar logic applies. Make sure the nature of the contact matches what you and they have agreed on.

## What gets logged

The app logs that a contact was reached out to — the fact of it, the time, the name of the contact you chose. It does **not** log the content of your message. If you sent a voice message, the app did not hear it; the SMS went through your phone's native messaging, not through our servers.

See [Privacy and your data](14_privacy_and_data.md) for more detail on what is and is not logged, and where.

## What to do after

Once you have reached out, you do not have to do anything else. You can go back to the app. You can close it. You can use another tool while you wait for a reply. You can just breathe and sit with the fact that you did the thing that was hard.

If the person does not reply immediately, that is not a verdict on you. Most people are not watching their phones. Try another contact, try another tool, or — if what is happening is moving past the scale of "reach out to a friend" — go to [Safety resources](13_safety_resources.md).

## What this is not

- Contact-a-human is not a crisis line. For a crisis, use the per-country hotlines in [Safety resources](13_safety_resources.md).
- Contact-a-human is not for casual texting. This is a specific tool for specific moments; treating it otherwise dilutes it.
- Contact-a-human is not how your clinician sees your data in general. Clinician sharing is a separate feature with its own scope and controls.

The whole design here is to make sure one of the most powerful interventions — another human — is never more than a few taps away at the moment you most need it.
