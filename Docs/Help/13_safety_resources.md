# Safety resources

If you are in a crisis right now, please go to the numbers below. The rest of this page can wait.

If you are having thoughts of suicide or self-harm, or if you are in immediate danger, please call, text, or visit one of the resources for your country. These people are trained for this and they are there for exactly this.

## United States

- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** — call or text **988**. 24/7, free, confidential.
- **Crisis Text Line** — text **HOME** to **741741**. 24/7.
- **Emergency:** 911.
- **The Trevor Project** (LGBTQ+ youth): call 1-866-488-7386 or text START to 678-678.
- **SAMHSA National Helpline** (substance use, mental health): 1-800-662-4357. 24/7, free, confidential.

## Canada

- **9-8-8 Suicide Crisis Helpline** — call or text **988**. 24/7, English and French.
- **Emergency:** 911.

## United Kingdom

- **Samaritans** — call **116 123**. 24/7, free.
- **SHOUT** — text **SHOUT** to **85258**. 24/7, free.
- **Emergency:** 999.

## France

## Elsewhere

If your country is not listed here, please use your local emergency number and consider the following international resources:

- **Befrienders Worldwide** (befrienders.org) — a global network of crisis help centers in more than 30 countries. They can direct you to a line that speaks your language.
- **International Association for Suicide Prevention** (iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres) — a directory of crisis centers worldwide.

The app's hotline directory is updated quarterly and reviewed by our Clinical Content Lead. If you notice a number that is no longer working, please report it through in-app support so we can fix it.

## When to use a hotline

- Thoughts of suicide or self-harm that feel serious to you — even if you are not sure if it is "bad enough" to call. It is. That is what the line is for.
- A crisis that feels beyond what a coping tool can hold.
- If you are not sure what you need but you need someone to talk to.
- If you are worried about someone else and you want to know what to do.

These lines are not only for a specific narrow definition of "crisis." They are for when things are hard and you are not sure what the next step is.

## What happens when you call

Every line is different, but in general:

- You will reach a trained person, usually a counselor or peer support specialist.
- The call is usually free and confidential.
- You do not have to have a plan. You do not have to have the right words. You can say *"I don't know where to start"* and they will start.
- If the situation requires additional response (e.g. a wellness check), they will discuss options with you, not surprise you.
- The call is not going into your medical record.

## Discipline OS's role

The app does not call anyone on your behalf. That is on purpose. Who to reach out to, and whether to reach out at all, is your decision. What the app can do is make sure the right numbers for where you are are one tap away at any time, even if the app is offline.

At T4 (the most serious tier — when a safety signal or an explicit request indicates that a crisis-level state is present), the app will display these resources prominently alongside any pre-configured emergency contact you have set up. The app will not bury this. It will not make you search.

## In the app

You can reach this page at any time by opening the app and going to Safety Resources. There is also a shortcut from the SOS screen. On the web at `crisis.disciplineos.com`, this page is always live, without needing to sign in.

## A note to anyone worried about someone else

If you are reading this because someone you care about is struggling, the best thing you can do is usually the simplest: ask them how they are, directly, and listen without trying to fix. Resources like the hotlines above can also be called by people who are concerned about a loved one — they can help you think about what to do next.
