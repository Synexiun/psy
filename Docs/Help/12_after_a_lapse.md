# What happens after a lapse

First, the most important thing we can tell you: **a lapse is not a failure.** It is a piece of data, and it is one of the most valuable moments this app has to work with.

This is not a platitude. It is a design principle.

## What the app will do

When you tap "I lapsed" (or, sometimes, the app infers a lapse from context and asks), several specific things happen in sequence. Here is what each one is, and why.

### 1. A compassion screen — first

The very first thing you will see is a single-screen message that is, intentionally, gentle. Something like: *"That's hard. You're here — that matters."*

Not: *"You broke your streak."* Not: *"Don't give up now."* Not any of the loss-aversion or shame-adjacent copy that is depressingly common in behavior-change apps.

This is because the research on post-lapse response is very consistent: **shame-based framing makes the next hour worse, not better.** The probability of a longer lapse increases when the internal story is "I failed." The probability of a recovery increases when the internal story is "That was hard, and I am still here."

The compassion screen is designed to interrupt the first story and reinforce the second.

### 2. A choice to do [a self-compassion break](10_self_compassion.md)

Optional. Two minutes. If your emotional state is rough — and it usually is — this is probably worth it before anything else.

### 3. Reduced-friction mode for 24 to 72 hours

After a lapse, the app adjusts:

- Your daily targets are temporarily lowered. The scale of challenge is calibrated to what is realistic in a post-lapse state, not your normal baseline.
- Check-ins become slightly more frequent and more supportive in tone.
- The tool library surfaces tools that are lower-effort and more restorative.
- Exposure suggestions are tempered — if the app usually surfaces "try getting back to the thing that was difficult," it will suggest that *later*, not right now.

You did not do anything to earn this mode. You just went through something hard, and the app is adapting.

### 4. Functional analysis — when you're ready

Within 48 hours, the app will offer you a structured reflection workflow. Not immediately. When the acute distress has settled and you can look back.

The workflow walks you through six questions:

1. **What was the situation?** Where, when, who, what?
2. **What was the cue?** What triggered the urge? A place, a person, a feeling, a time of day?
3. **What were your thoughts?** What was the internal voice saying?
4. **What did you do?** Just facts.
5. **What was the outcome?** How did you feel an hour later, a day later?
6. **What is one alternative?** Looking back with less heat in the moment, what is one thing you might have done differently?

This is the classic CBT functional-analysis structure (Beck, 1979). It works because it takes the messy, cloudy experience of the lapse and sorts it into pieces you can look at clearly.

Doing this within the first 48 hours produces the best data — both for your own learning and for the app's pattern recognition. Your memory of the specifics is still good.

You can skip it. You can come back to it. You can do a voice version instead of typing. The workflow is a resource, not an obligation.

### 5. The two streaks

On your dashboard, there are two streaks:

- **Continuous streak**: days since your last lapse. When a lapse happens, this one honestly resets. It is still on the dashboard, but de-emphasized. It is not the main picture.
- **Resilience streak**: the count of urges you have handled. **This one does not reset on a lapse.** It cannot. Every urge you handled is still an urge you handled. That is the number that grows over time. That is the one we want you looking at.

This design choice is deliberate. Most behavior-change apps use only the continuous streak, and it teaches a damaging lesson: that the only thing that counts is perfection. In practice, that framing makes lapses worse — because the story becomes "I broke the streak, so why bother."

The resilience streak tells the truer story. You are building a repertoire. Each urge handled is one more rep.

### 6. Updated patterns

Your lapse goes into the pattern analytics — anonymized, in aggregate, for *your own* pattern view. The trigger context, the state signals leading up to it, the tool (if any) that was attempted — all of this feeds back. Within a week or two, you may see a new insight: *"Lapses in your history tend to cluster on Fridays after 6pm"* or *"The last three lapses came after nights with less than 5 hours of sleep."*

These are not accusations. They are data. The point of seeing them is that they give you something to work with. Maybe a new [if-then plan](07_implementation_intentions.md) is the next thing, targeted at the pattern.

## What the app will not do

- The app will not notify anyone that you lapsed. Not your contacts, not your clinician, unless you have explicitly set up notification for those events with that scope.
- The app will not punish you. There is no downgrade, no lost badge, no "days clean" reset that you see first.
- The app will not tell you what you "should have done." The functional-analysis workflow has *you* identify alternatives, not the app.
- The app will not treat a lapse as a reason to push harder. It will do the opposite.

## What you can do, if you want

- Tap [contact-a-human](09_contact_a_human.md) and reach out to one of your people. The post-lapse window is exactly what that feature is built for.
- Read [self-compassion break](10_self_compassion.md).
- Leave a voice note in the journal — unstructured, as you please. No one will hear it but you.
- Do nothing for a while, then come back.

## A final note

If what happened feels beyond "a lapse" — if it involves thoughts of self-harm, or something that feels like a crisis rather than a slip — go to [Safety resources](13_safety_resources.md). That is a different situation and it has different support.

For everything else: you are here. You tapped the button. The hardest part of the loop has already happened. The rest is gentler than it feels right now.
